Reading Assessment: Student Guide

Learn how to read critically and answer comprehension questions effectively.

What You're Going to Do

You will read a passage and answer questions that test how well you understand what you read. But this isn't just about remembering facts—it's about thinking deeply about what the author is saying and how they say it.

The 4 Skills You'll Practice

1. Find the Central Message

What it means: Every passage has a MAIN POINT—the ONE big idea the author wants you to understand.

How to do it:

  • Ask yourself: "If I had to explain this passage in ONE sentence, what would I say?"
  • Look at the beginning and end—authors often state their main point there
  • Don't get distracted by details—focus on the BIG picture

2. Follow the Author's Reasoning

What it means: Good authors don't just state their point—they BUILD an argument with supporting ideas that connect logically.

How to do it:

  • Notice how the passage is organized (beginning → middle → end)
  • Ask: "Why did the author put this part HERE?"
  • Look for the structure: Does it tell a story? Explain something? Try to convince you?

3. Evaluate the Evidence

What it means: Authors use EVIDENCE to support their points. Your job is to judge: Is this evidence strong? Does it actually prove what the author claims?

How to do it:

  • When you see a claim, ask: "What proof does the author give?"
  • Look for: facts, statistics, examples, expert opinions, personal experiences
  • Ask: "Does this evidence ACTUALLY support the point, or is it weak?"

4. Make Connections

What it means: Great readers connect what they read to OTHER texts, to their OWN lives, and to the WORLD.

How to do it:

  • Text-to-Text: "This reminds me of another book/article I read..."
  • Text-to-Self: "This connects to my own experience when..."
  • Text-to-World: "This relates to what's happening in the world because..."

Step-by-Step: How to Complete a Reading Assessment

1

Preview (30 seconds)

Read the title, look at the passage type, and skim the questions so you know what to look for.

2

First Read (focus on understanding)

Read the whole passage once to understand the MAIN IDEA. Don't worry about every detail yet.

3

Second Read (focus on details)

Read again, noticing HOW the author builds their argument, key evidence, and connections to themes.

4

Answer Questions Strategically

Read each question carefully, go BACK to the passage for evidence, eliminate wrong answers, choose the BEST answer.

5

Check Your Work

Did you answer every question? Did you go back to the passage for evidence?

Reading Like a Detective: Questions to Ask Yourself

About the Main Message:

  • What is the author's ONE main point?
  • Why did the author write this?
  • What does the author want me to think, feel, or do?

About Structure:

  • How is this organized?
  • Why did the author put things in this order?
  • How does each paragraph connect to the main point?

About Evidence:

  • What proof does the author give?
  • Is this evidence convincing?
  • Are there any gaps in the reasoning?

About Deeper Meaning:

  • What is the author NOT saying directly?
  • What can I infer from clues?
  • How does this connect to bigger ideas?

Vocabulary Strategy: Context Clues

When you find a word you don't know:

  1. Read around it - Look at the sentence before and after
  2. Look for definitions - Sometimes the author explains it
  3. Find synonyms - Look for similar words nearby
  4. Use logic - What word would MAKE SENSE here?
  5. Check the tone - Is it positive, negative, or neutral?
Example:
"The waters were treacherous, requiring a lighthouse to guide ships safely."

You might not know "treacherous," but the context tells you the waters are dangerous (that's why ships need guidance).

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Answering from memory - Always go BACK to the passage
  • ❌ Choosing the first "good" answer - Read ALL options first
  • ❌ Adding your own opinions - Stick to what the TEXT says
  • ❌ Ignoring the question type - Different questions need different approaches
  • ❌ Rushing - Take time to think deeply

Achievement Levels: What They Mean

Level Score What It Means
Level 4 80-100% Exceeds expectations—you're thinking deeply and making strong connections
Level 3 70-79% Meets expectations—solid understanding of the text
Level 2 60-69% Approaching expectations—you understand the basics but need to go deeper
Level 1 50-59% Needs improvement—focus on finding the main idea and supporting evidence

You've Got This!

Remember:

  • Reading is THINKING, not just seeing words
  • Every passage has a structure—find it
  • Evidence matters—always go back to the text
  • Connections make you a stronger reader

Good luck!
