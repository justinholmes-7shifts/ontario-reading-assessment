Teacher Assessment Guide

Detailed rubrics and assessment criteria for evaluating student work in reading and writing assessments.

Quick Reference: What to Look For

Reading Assessment

  1. Can student identify the ONE main idea?
  2. Can student recognize HOW the author builds their argument?
  3. Can student evaluate which evidence is strongest?
  4. Can student make inferences from implied information?

Writing Assessment

  1. Is the main message clear in paragraph 1?
  2. Does each paragraph answer "why?" or "how?" about the main message?
  3. Are points different from each other (no overlap)?
  4. Together, do points cover everything important?

The Single Most Important Question: "Can I follow the student's thinking from start to finish?"

Reading Rubric by Question Type

Central Message Questions

"What is the main theme/central message of this passage?"

Level Criteria
4 Correctly identifies overarching theme; understands how details support main idea; distinguishes main idea from supporting details
3 Correctly identifies main theme; understands general relationship to supporting ideas
2 Identifies a theme but may confuse it with a supporting detail or secondary idea
1 Unable to identify main theme; focuses on minor details or misunderstands passage

Evidence Evaluation Questions

"Which detail BEST supports the claim that...?"

Level Criteria
4 Selects strongest evidence; can articulate WHY it's stronger than alternatives
3 Selects appropriate evidence; understands connection to claim
2 Selects evidence that partially supports the claim or selects weaker over stronger options
1 Selects evidence that doesn't support the claim or misunderstands the relationship

Inference Questions

"What can you infer about...?"

Level Criteria
4 Makes sophisticated inferences supported by multiple textual clues; can explain reasoning
3 Makes reasonable inferences supported by text; understands implicit meaning
2 Makes inferences but may over-interpret or under-support with textual evidence
1 Unable to make inferences; sticks only to explicit statements or makes unsupported guesses

Part 2: Writing Assessment Rubrics

Writing Assessment Categories (25% each)

1. Main Message & Focus

Question: Is the main point clear from the start?

Level Score Criteria Look For
4 80-100% Main message is crystal clear from opening; reader immediately understands the point Main point in para 1, specific not vague, no guessing required
3 70-79% Main message is clear and stated early in the writing Main point clearly present, stated in introduction, mostly specific
2 60-69% Main message is present but may be buried or unclear Main point exists, may be in middle/end, somewhat vague
1 50-59% Main message is missing or very difficult to identify No clear main point, multiple competing ideas, reader confused

2. Logical Structure

Question: Do supporting points clearly connect to the main message?

Level Score Criteria Look For
4 80-100% Each paragraph directly answers 'why?' or 'how?' about the main message Clear paragraph purposes, logical flow, strong transitions
3 70-79% Supporting points connect to main message with clear logic Mostly clear connections, good organization, minor gaps
2 60-69% Some connection between points and main message, but logic unclear Some connections present, logic gaps, unclear transitions
1 50-59% Points seem disconnected or don't clearly support the main message Unclear connections, random organization, reader gets lost

3. Grouping & Completeness

Question: Are ideas organized without overlap? Do they cover everything important?

Level Score Criteria Look For
4 80-100% Ideas perfectly grouped; no overlap between points; nothing important missing Each point distinct, no repetition, comprehensive coverage
3 70-79% Ideas well-grouped with minimal overlap; covers main points Mostly distinct points, minor overlap, covers key points
2 60-69% Some grouping but ideas may overlap or important points missing Some overlap, missing arguments, partial coverage
1 50-59% Ideas jumbled, repetitive, or major points missing Significant repetition, major gaps, poor grouping

4. Conventions & Clarity

Question: Is the writing clear and error-free?

Level Score Criteria Look For
4 80-100% Virtually no errors; writing is exceptionally clear Error-free, excellent flow, varied sentences
3 70-79% Few errors that don't impede understanding Minor errors, clear expression, good variety
2 60-69% Some errors that may impede understanding Noticeable errors, some confusion, repetitive
1 50-59% Frequent errors that significantly impede understanding Many errors, hard to understand, basic vocabulary

Quick Assessment Checklist

Main Message & Focus

  • ☐ Main point present? Where?
  • ☐ Specific or vague?
  • ☐ Reader must guess?

Logical Structure

  • ☐ Each paragraph = ONE purpose?
  • ☐ Points answer "why/how"?
  • ☐ Clear transitions?

Grouping & Completeness

  • ☐ Points are different?
  • ☐ Any overlap/repetition?
  • ☐ Missing anything obvious?

Conventions

  • ☐ Spelling/grammar errors?
  • ☐ Sentence variety?
  • ☐ Word count met?

Sample Feedback Templates

Level 4 Feedback

"Your main message is crystal clear from the start, and each paragraph directly supports it with distinct, non-overlapping reasons. Excellent logical structure!"

Level 3 Feedback

"Good clear thesis. Your points support your main message well. Try moving your thesis earlier and making your transitions more explicit to strengthen your structure."

Level 2 Feedback

"I found your main point in paragraph 3—move it to paragraph 1. Also, points 2 and 3 overlap (they're both about [X]). How can you make them truly different?"

Level 1 Feedback

"I'm not sure what your main message is. Try completing this sentence: 'The ONE thing I want my reader to understand is...' Then make sure every paragraph supports that one thing."

Red Flags to Watch For

Reading Red Flags

  • Consistently misidentifies main ideas → Focus on distinguishing detail from theme
  • Can't use context clues → Vocabulary instruction needed
  • Over-interprets or invents → Focus on text evidence

Writing Red Flags

  • Main message buried or missing → Direct instruction on thesis placement
  • Points repeat same idea → Teach "different types of arguments"
  • Points don't connect → Practice "why?" test
  • Writes without planning → Enforce Think First framework
